Machine Learning-Based Prediction of Protein–Ligand Binding Affinity¶

Project Overview¶

  • Focuses on predicting protein–ligand binding affinity, an essential aspect of drug discovery.

  • Protein features were extracted from amino acid composition and reduced using PCA for dimensionality reduction.

  • Ligand features were derived from SMILES strings, converted into molecular fingerprints, and standardized.

  • Combined protein and ligand features were used as input for a Random Forest Regressor.

  • Model performance was evaluated using Mean Squared Error (MSE) and R² Score.

Visualizations included:¶

  • Scatter plot of Actual vs. Predicted Affinity.

  • Residual distribution plot to analyze prediction errors.

  • Residual histogram to examine error frequency.

  • Demonstrates the integration of bioinformatics and machine learning for modeling molecular interactions.

  • Provides a computational approach that can aid drug design and discovery by identifying potential binders.

amino_acids = 'ACDEFGHIKLMNPQRSTVWY'

def aa_composition(seq):
    seq = seq.upper()
    length = len(seq)
    return [seq.count(aa)/length for aa in amino_acids]

protein_features = np.array(data['Protein_Seq'].apply(aa_composition).tolist())

Step 5: Protein Feature Dimensionality Reduction (PCA)¶

This step applies Principal Component Analysis (PCA) to reduce the high-dimensional protein features into two principal components (PC1 & PC2). The scatter plot visualizes protein sequences in a 2D space, making it easier to observe clustering and patterns.

🔹 Why PCA?

Reduces noise and redundancy in data.

Helps in visualizing high-dimensional biological features.

Improves computational efficiency for machine learning models.

Step 13 : Histogram of residuals¶

This step plots a histogram of the residuals to visualize their distribution. A roughly normal (bell-shaped) distribution centered around zero indicates that the model errors are balanced, whereas skewed or wide distributions may suggest bias or high variance in predictions.
